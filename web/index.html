rota2ics

Convert a UK railway-staff rota PDF into a calendar (.ics) file for Google Calendar, Apple Calendar or Outlook. Everything runs in your browser — your PDF is never uploaded.

On iPhone or iPad: open this page in Safari for the smoothest hand-off to the Calendar app.

1 Choose your rota PDF

2 Pick your Link and row

Not sure which row you’re on? Match it to your shifts

Pick the row whose first few days match your roster for the week you chose. Tap it to select.

What do these mean?
  • Link — the rota number printed at the top of your PDF (e.g. “Link 1”).
  • Your row this week (Wk) — the numbered row of that Link covering the week starting on the Sunday you choose. The row advances automatically and wraps back to 1 at the end of the cycle.
  • Sunday of that week — must be a Sunday. If you pick another day we’ll snap back to the previous Sunday.
  • Weeks to generate — how far ahead to project events. 26 ≈ 6 months, 52 = a year.

3 Options

What do these mean?
  • Calendar name — the name shown when the .ics is imported.
  • Title prefix — prepended to every event title (e.g. “Work: BM 112”).
Rest days (FD / RD)
What is FD / RD?

FD = Free Day (some operators print RD) on the rota. When ticked, each rest day appears as a transparent all-day event so the day looks free in your calendar but is clearly labelled.

As Ordered (AO)

AO — you’re on duty but the actual diagram is assigned on the day. The PDF prints nominal sign-on / sign-off times.

Spare (SP)

SP = Spare — other operators’ name for AO. On duty but the diagram is assigned on the day; the PDF prints nominal sign-on / sign-off times.

Route Refresher (RB)

RB — route familiarisation turn. The PDF prints sign-on / sign-off times.

About the .ics file

Times are written as floating local times, so each shift always displays in the viewer’s local time zone (perfect for UK use across BST/GMT).
